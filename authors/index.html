I am a PhD candidate at the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Washington, coadvised by Prof. Maryam Fazel and Prof. Simon S. Du. I am also a Visiting Research Scientist at Meta FAIR under a 2-year Meta AI Mentorship Fellowship, working with Lin Xiao.

My research develops methods, benchmarks, and theory for personalizing AI systems to diverse users. As LLMs are deployed at scale, the bottleneck shifts from model capability to understanding what each user actually needs. I build evaluation frameworks that expose where personalization fails (PrefDisco), inference-time methods that adapt without retraining (LoRe, PEP), and robustness tools for agents in adversarial settings (DoomArena). My work spans personalization, RLHF, preference elicitation, agent security, and multi-agent coordination, with publications at ICLR, NeurIPS, COLM, AISTATS, AAAI, and ECAI.

Prior to UW, I was a Research Engineer at Singapore Management University working with Prof. Arunesh Sinha and Prof. Pradeep Varakantham. I completed my undergraduate studies at IIT Kanpur in Computer Science and Engineering.
